There are too many "best tools 2026" listicles written by people who never opened the apps they recommend.

**Pick Rack is the opposite of that.**

I'm an indie maker who's tested, broken, and replaced hundreds of SaaS tools across years of side projects — paid and free, AI and classical, polished and janky. This site is where I share **what actually worked**.

## What you'll find here

Three buckets, all free or generous-free-tier:

- **PDF tools** — converters, extractors, mergers, OCR. I cover what works, what doesn't, and when paid is worth it.
- **AI tools** — the LLM, image, audio, and writing tools I keep going back to (and the ones that disappointed).
- **Dev stack** — frameworks, deploy targets, monitoring, and the boring infra that actually matters for solo makers and small teams.

## What you won't find

- "Top 50 tools" articles. Nobody uses 50 tools.
- Sponsored content disguised as reviews. (Affiliate links exist, always disclosed, never the reason for a recommendation.)
- AI-generated filler. Each piece is written by a human who used the thing.

## How this works

- **Free reading**, no paywall, no email gate
- **Affiliate links** disclosed at the top of any post that has them
- **Display ads** keep the lights on
- **No newsletter** yet — when there's enough signal, I'll add one
